# Drop culture, hype & the launch playbook

**Purpose:** How apparel brands manufacture demand — drop mechanics, scarcity psychology, and the cross-channel launch sequence. This shapes the *site*, not just the marketing: a drop brand's homepage is a countdown and a list, not a catalog.

**Harvest / review:** 2026-06

---

## The four levers of hype

| Lever | What it means | Design implication |
|-------|---------------|--------------------|
| **Scarcity** | Produce less than demand; limited quantities/sizes | Show stock/edition limits; "limited run" language; sold-out states are features, not failures |
| **Exclusivity** | Unique designs, collabs, members-only access | Early-access tiers; password/insider URLs; "list-only" framing |
| **Urgency** | Time-boxed releases, sometimes unannounced | Countdown timers; a concrete drop window (even "Summer 2026" beats nothing) |
| **Community** | Frequent drops build a returning, connected fanbase | Capture the contact (email/SMS) first; nurture between drops |

Pioneered by Supreme; now the default model for new streetwear/DTC labels. Shifts brands from **discount-based** scarcity (markdowns) to **product-based** scarcity (it's just rare) — protects margin and brand.

---

## The launch sequence (pre-launch → drop)

1. **Stand up a coming-soon page early** and start capturing the list before anything is for sale. See [`coming-soon-and-prelaunch.md`](coming-soon-and-prelaunch.md).
2. **Tease** weeks ahead — high-quality teaser photos/short clips on IG/TikTok; build anticipation, don't always reveal the date (surprise is a lever).
3. **Reward the list** — early access + a launch-day discount for subscribers. The captured contact is the asset.
4. **Coordinate the drop across channels simultaneously** — email + SMS + social fire together at drop time. A launch video + 15–30 creator reposts in the first hour is the repeatable amplification block.
5. **Make it an event** — aim to sell out fast; the sellout itself becomes the next round of buzz.
6. **Nurture between drops** — the community lever; keep the list warm so the next drop starts hot.

---

## Site/architecture implications

- **Capacity**: real drops spike traffic hard (sites crash, sell out in minutes). Static/CDN-served pages (Cloudflare Pages) and a queue/inventory strategy matter for the *store*; the coming-soon page just needs to not fall over.
- **Owned channel first**: Gen Z/millennials prefer buying direct from the brand's own store/site over marketplaces — own the relationship and the data.
- **The contact list is the product** pre-launch. Every design decision on a coming-soon page should protect the one conversion: get the email/phone.

---

## Anti-patterns

| Avoid | Why |
|-------|-----|
| Launching to silence (no pre-built list) | The drop has no audience; hype needs a primed crowd |
| "Coming soon" with no date/window | Kills urgency — the strongest lever |
| Permanent discounts to drive sales | Trains customers to wait; erodes the scarcity/brand premium |
| Revealing everything early | Spends the surprise lever; nothing left to anticipate |
